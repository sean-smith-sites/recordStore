What Should I Buy?

There are many formats for records to come in, here is a quick list of the differences between them.

  1. 45s- Also Known as 7 inches. They usually contain a single track on both sides of the disc. Most 45s have a larger spindle hole and will take a small circular adaptor to play. The smaller records spin at 45 rotations a minute, hence their name.

  2. 12 inch Records- This format is similar to a 45 in the fact they usually contain singles and not whole albums. You can usually find remixes of songs on 12 inch records. This format is great for DJing and record scratching.

  3. 12 inch long play (LPs)- This format is the standard and usually what you think of when it comes to records. This format contains whole albums hence the name long play.

  4. 180 gram 12 LP- These are heavy weight pressing of records, usually reissues, that weigh 180 grams. With this you get a sturdy and structurally soundrecord that makes for great pressing and audiophile quality sound

  5. 78s-78s were the first format of records to come out. They were shallaced, heavy and brittle. Because of that, they use a different needle to play. Do not play them on your standard record player. The format came out early in the 20th century and a nation was hooked on being able to enjoy music at home.
